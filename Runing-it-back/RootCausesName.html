Root cause

Lower income communities face food inequality due to little to no access to fresh produce and grocery stores in close proximity to where they live, these places are called food deserts.

Evidence

  • 43.1% of California census tracts are low-income, and 24.5% are low-access.
  • 19 mil­lion peo­ple or 6.2% of the nation’s total pop­u­la­tion had lim­it­ed access to a super­mar­ket or gro­cery store

    • Explanation

  • people with low access are greatly impacted by the effects of food deserts learning to higher risks of obesity, and health problems related to diet because they don’t have access to fresh and healthy food.
  • A large amount of people in the u.s are affected by a problem that can be avoided, by opening more produce stores, or creating sustainably food options.

    • Implication

  • I learned the effects and causes of food deserts and how they hurt low income communities the most. Things that can help are sustainable access to fresh produce, or creating small scale community farms that can provide fresh produce to communities in need.
