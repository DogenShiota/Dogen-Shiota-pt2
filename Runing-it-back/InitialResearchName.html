Problem Statment

Many communities within the bay area are in food deserts, unable to have access fresh produce and foods, putting people at increased risk of obesity, diabetes, and other weight-related conditions.

Need Statement

We need to provide better options and alternatives for access to fresh produce and affordable organic foods.

  • More then 900 neighborhoods are considered to be in food deserts in the bay area.
  • Food deserts effect low income areas the most, effecting people of color disproportionally.
  • Food deserts are offten caused by food insecurity, racial segregation, proximity to supermarkets, access to a vehicle, and various other social factors
  • About 23.5 million people live in food deserts in the U.S and 1 million of them being from California.
